FOOD

From coffee to cocktails, check out Penn students’ favorite place to eat and drink. Here are the Best of Penn 2020 winners for the category Food & Drink.

BEST BRUNCH

Sabrina’s Cafe

Looking for a brunch spot on campus? Look no further than Sabrina’s Cafe, this year’s pick for Best Brunch Penn. Located on 227 N 34th St, Sabrina’s is convenient for both Penn and Drexel students looking for a delicious brunch or breakfast. Make sure to check out the sweet and simple breakfast plates.

ADDRESS: 227 N 34th St.

HOURS: 8AM-3PM, Closed on Tuesday

BEST VEGAN

HipCityVeg

Check out HipCityVeg, the Best of Penn winner for a vegan restaurant. Located on 214 S 40th St, HipCityVeg boasts an entirely vegan menu from appetizer to entree, complete with brownies and banana whips for dessert! HipCityVeg also has an extensive breakfast menu, which includes bagels, cereals, and yogurt parfait.

ADDRESS: 214 S 40th St.

HOURS: 11AM-9PM

BEST PIZZA

&pizza

Grab a slice of pizza, or rather a personal pizza, at &pizza, this year’s winner of Best Pizza at Penn. &pizza, located at 3925 Walnut St, allows you to customize your individual pie or choose from a menu of pre-created pizzas. &pizza’s modern, thin-crust, oval style and convenience is what makes them a student body favorite.

ADDRESS: 3925 Walnut St.

HOURS: Sun-Thurs: 11AM-10PM, Fri-Sat: 11AM-11PM

BEST FOOD TRUCK

Penn Halal Gyro

For a quick bite on the go, visit the Halal Food Truck in University City. The food truck serves sizable portions of traditional halal food with the option of beef, chicken, or lamb. The truck also offers platters with deals that include drinks and sides. Make sure to add the white sauce to your platter, but try the hot sauce at your own risk!

ADDRESS: 237 S 40th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104

HOURS: 10:30AM-8PM

BEST KOREAN FOOD

Koreana

QUARANTINE

FASTEST FOOD DELIVERY

YOUR
PICK

UberEats

For the quickest and safest way to get food during the pandemic, look toward UberEats. A favorite of Penn students during COVID, UberEats delivers food from almost any restaurant within a 20 mile radius. UberEats also offers non-contact delivery, so you can stay safe while enjoying your local University City favorites.

FAVORITE ONLINE VIDEO CONFERENCING PLATFORM

YOUR
PICK

Zoom

Looking to connect virtually with friends and family? Penn students turn to Zoom in the age of COVID for videoconferencing. Widely used as the virtual classroom for the university, zoom also offers a chance for students to connect with their peers while the environment at Penn remains virtual.

BEST QUARANTINE GAME OR ACTIVITIES

YOUR
PICK

Among Us

With other activities shut down or postponed, Penn students turned to Among Us to keep themselves entertained during the pandemic. Among Us is a multiplayer online game that pits “crewmates” against each other in a race to determine which one(s) of them is the “imposter.” Among Us also makes it easy to play with friends, via a code or private lobby.

BEST BINGES

YOUR
PICK

Avatar

Downtime during quarantine manifested itself in binge-watching Netflix for so many individuals. While Tiger King and the Office were both popular choices, Penn students most enjoyed spending time with Aang on his journey to unite the four nations.

BEST GYM

YOUR
PICK

OrangeTheory Fitness

With Pottruck shut down for the Fall of 2020, Penn students ventured to University City gyms in order to stay physically fit. OrangeTheory Fitness, on 37th and Market, is the preferred choice of students during the pandemic. OrangeTheory has a unique, heart rate-based approach to fitness that aims to optimize a workout with High-Intensity Interval Training. It’s a HIIT!

BEST OUTDOOR DINING

YOUR
PICK

Copabanana

For the best outdoor dining experience in University City during the COVID pandemic, visit Copabanana on 40th and Spruce. Affectionately nicknamed “Copa” by the student body, the restaurant has capitalized on outdoor dining by setting tables in a roped-off area on 40th street. The margaritas and burgers are student favorites, so make sure to head over before the winter.

BEST SELF-CARE ROUTINE

YOUR
PICK

Sleep

To combat the stress of the pandemic, Penn students turned to sleep. Instead of a bath or a day at the spa, Penn students felt that getting more sleep was most effective when dealing with COVID-induced anxiety or pressure.
